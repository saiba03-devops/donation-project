Help Stop Child Labour and Prevent Child Labour in India

At Donation, we work to create a safe and protective environment for every child. Our mission is to prevent child labour, protect children from exploitation, and help them continue their education with dignity.

Our Program Against Child Labour

Children exposed to unsafe work environments face serious consequences that affect their physical, emotional, mental and social development. We work with communities to help rescue child labour victims and support their return to school.

Here are the main focus areas of our child protection program:

Access To Government Benefits

Helping vulnerable children complete their education by getting access to scholarships, education materials and government support.

Connecting Parents With Job Opportunities

Supporting families with livelihood opportunities so children do not have to work and can continue their education.

Organising Awareness Rallies

Mobilising communities and spreading awareness to bring children back to school and protect them from labour.

FAQs

How can I contribute to an NGO for child labour in India?

You can contribute by supporting awareness campaigns, donating towards children’s education and protection programs, or volunteering your time.

What are the challenges faced by NGOs fighting against child labour?

Child labour NGOs often face challenges such as poverty, lack of awareness, limited access to education and social acceptance of child labour.

What steps can I take to help stop child labour?

You can raise awareness in your community, support education campaigns, donate to trusted child welfare programs, and encourage children to stay in school.

Why should you support an NGO fighting against child labour?

Supporting child labour prevention helps children get education, safety, opportunities and protection from unsafe work environments.

How does support help prevent child labour at the grassroots level?

Support helps strengthen awareness programs, connect families to government schemes, provide education support, and protect children from exploitation.
